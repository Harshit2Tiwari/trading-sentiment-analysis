Dataset: May 2023 – May 2025  |  211,224 trades

Overview

Data Science Assignment — Sentiment vs. Performance Study

This report analyses 211,224 trades from Hyperliquid against the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index to understand how market sentiment affects trader behaviour and profitability. Two datasets were merged on trade date: the historical Hyperliquid trade log and the daily Fear & Greed classification (Extreme Fear → Extreme Greed).

Key dimensions explored: total and per-trade PnL by sentiment, win rates, trade volume patterns, asset-level performance across sentiment regimes, and a comparison of top vs. bottom traders.

7. Conclusion & Strategy Recommendations

What the data tells us

The analysis of 211,224 Hyperliquid trades shows a clear and consistent relationship between market sentiment and trader profitability. Greed-phase trading outperforms Fear-phase trading by a significant margin — not just in total PnL, but also in average per-trade returns.

  • Sentiment-gated entry: Traders who enter positions during Greed periods (index ≥ 50) earn on average $64–$78 per trade, compared to $27–$29 during Fear. A simple sentiment filter can meaningfully improve expected value.
  • Asset selection matters: ETH shows the highest sensitivity to sentiment — +$323/trade during Greed vs. −$184/trade during Extreme Greed (contrarian signal). SOL is consistently profitable across Greed and Neutral phases.
  • Win rate is not alpha: The top earner achieved $2.14M with only a 33.7% win rate. The key differentiator is position sizing and cutting losses early, not trying to win more often.
  • Avoid overtrading in Fear: Fear periods see the highest volume ($622M) but the worst win rates (36.87%). This suggests emotional, reactive trading during drawdowns is a primary driver of losses.
  • December 2024 as a case study: A single month generated $3M+ in aggregate PnL — coinciding with peak Greed sentiment during the Bitcoin ETF-driven rally. Trend-following in high-Greed environments has historically paid off.
